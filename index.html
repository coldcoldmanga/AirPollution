What is AQI

The Air Quality Index (AQI) is a measurement of air pollutant concentrations in ambient air pollution and their associated health risks. The AQI typically ranges from 0 to 500, with lower values indicating better air quality and higher values signaling increased pollution levels.

Healthy 0-50
Air Quality is considered satisfactory, and air pollution posses little or no risk.
Moderate 51-100
Sensitive individuals should avoid outdoor activity as they may experience respitory symptoms.
Unhealthy for Sensitive Group 101-150
General public and sensitive individuals in particular are at risk to experience irritation and respitory problems.
Unhealthy 151-200
Increased likehood of adverse effects and aggravation to the heart and lungs among general public.
Very Unhealthy 201-300
General public will be noticeably affected. Sensitive groups should restrict outdoor activities.
Harzadous 300+
General public at high risk of experiencing strong irritations and advere health effects. Should avoid outdoor activities.

Causes of Air Pollution in Malaysia

Industries
Industry includes pollution from facilities such as manufacturing factories, mines, and oil refineries as well as coal power plants and boilers for heat and power generation.
Development Activities
Development activities such as construction, industrial operations, and deforestation emit pollutants like particulate matter and gases, exacerbating air pollution.
Transportation
Air pollution from transport refers primarily to fuel combustion in motor vehicles, such as in cars, trucks, trains, planes, and ships. Transport emissions are a major contributor to elevated levels of fine particulate matter (PM2.5), ozone, and nitrogen dioxide (NO2).
Wild Fires and Open Burning
The burning of plant matter emits large amounts of pollutants, as does burning other solid fuels like coal. Burning organic material emits particulate matter (PM), nitrogen oxides (NOx), carbon monoxide (CO), sulfur dioxide (SO2), lead, mercury, and other hazardous air pollutants (HAPs). These fires may occur organically, accidentally, or intentionally.

Solutions to Improve Air Quality

Promote the Use of Public Transportation and Carpooling

Encouraging the use of public transportation and carpooling can significantly reduce the number of vehicles on the road, which in turn lowers emissions from cars and trucks. Public transportation systems, such as buses, trains, and subways, are designed to move large numbers of people efficiently, reducing the overall carbon footprint per passenger. Carpooling, or ride-sharing, is another effective strategy where individuals share a ride to a common destination. This not only cuts down on the number of vehicles but also reduces traffic congestion, leading to less idling and lower emissions. Governments and organizations can support this measure by investing in robust public transit systems, creating carpool lanes, and offering incentives for those who choose to carpool or use public transport.

Promote Renewable Energy Sources
Transitioning to renewable energy sources such as solar, wind, hydro, and geothermal can drastically reduce air pollution. Unlike fossil fuels, renewable energy sources do not emit harmful pollutants or greenhouse gases during energy production. By investing in and promoting the use of clean energy technologies, governments and organizations can decrease reliance on coal, oil, and natural gas. This can be achieved through various incentives like subsidies for renewable energy projects, tax credits for households and businesses that install solar panels or wind turbines, and implementing policies that encourage the development of green energy infrastructure. Additionally, educating the public about the benefits of renewable energy can drive consumer demand and further support the shift towards a cleaner energy future.
Implement and Enforce Stricter Emission Regulations
Implementing and enforcing stricter emission regulations for industries, vehicles, and power plants is crucial in controlling the amount of pollutants released into the air. These regulations can include setting limits on the amount of harmful gases and particulate matter that can be emitted, mandating the use of cleaner technologies, and requiring regular maintenance and inspection of emission control systems. Governments can also incentivize companies to adopt greener practices through subsidies, tax breaks, or grants for those who invest in renewable energy sources or upgrade to cleaner technologies. By ensuring compliance through regular monitoring and imposing penalties for violations, these regulations can significantly reduce air pollution levels and improve air quality.
